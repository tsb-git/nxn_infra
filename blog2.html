Asphalt Maintenance 101: Extend the Life of Your Parking Lot with These Proven Tips

The asphalt parking lots are quite common in industrial and commercial areas and are a significant investment for business owners. In order to ensure that the asphalt parking spaces last longer, it is mandatory to set up a maintenance routine and follow it. Repaving the parking lot is inconvenient and expensive, and in order to keep the parking lot in excellent condition, appropriate maintenance is mandatory.

Proven tips to extend the life of your asphalt parking lot:

Having a parking lot that is smooth, even, and with clearly marked lines is essential for the commercial property. A rundown parking lot with cracks, potholes, and near-invisible lines can result in vehicle damage and also set a poor impression of the property. An attractive and well-maintained parking lot is more pleasant for driving, while creating an exceptional impression on the property. Some proven tips to improve the life of the asphalt parking lot are as follows.

1. Regular cleaning:

Regular cleaning is the first step in asphalt maintenance. It is important to clear away trash, debris, and even dirt from the driveways, private roads, and parking lots to extend their life. Clean parking lots will improve curb appeal and will also discourage littering. Also, a clean parking lot can deter rodents and pest problems. Leaving dirt and yard debris on the pavement surface may not seem like a big deal, but adding water can erode the asphalt surface. Erosion will result in asphalt cracks, substructure damage, etc., which can affect large areas of the parking lot or driveway if left alone.

2. Preventive maintenance:

Preventive treatments for asphalt pavements will help improve the quality of the pavement surface and extend its life. Preventive maintenance activities will include the conventional treatments such as chip sealing, crack sealing, rut filling, fog sealing, and applying thin overlays. Some of the emerging technologies, such as ultra-thin wearing courses, microsufacing applications, etc., are also considered in preventive maintenance. Some treatments aim at applying a new wearing surface for the pavement. For instance, a fog seal provides a new wearing surface, though it has a lower friction level than the original surface.

3. Filling and sealing asphalt cracks:

Moisture can weaken the substructure of the asphalt and is indeed the biggest cause of deterioration of asphalt pavement. The cracks that are bigger than ⅛ inch should be filled and sealed to prevent water from getting into the asphalt and creating further damage. Asphalt pavement with sealed cracks can hold up for a longer time than pavements with unsealed cracks. Larger cracks with edge deterioration, worn pavement, and pavement areas with many cracks will require individual filling techniques.

4. Repair the damaged asphalt:

If the asphalt cracks are too big and if the cracks are too wide or are extensive to be effectively filled or sealed to prevent future damage, they will have to be removed and patched. The asphalt repair and maintenance work will take much longer. It will also cost much less to have a portion of the asphalt driveway or parking lot removed and replaced than it takes to replace the entire asphalt parking lot.

5. Protect the asphalt parking with seal coating:

An important measure to extend the life of the parking lot is to perform asphalt seal coating. As the oil in the new asphalt pavement oxidizes, the pavement can get harder and harder. It can also become brittle if it is left to oxidize for a long time. For these reasons, the newly laid asphalt pavements should be seal-coated in their first year and re-sealed every three to five years. Seal coating offers an additional layer of protection from the sun, oil, water, and other harmful elements. It will also make the driveway look uniform in color with a dark blacktop finish.

6. Re-stripe parking lots:

Apart from asphalt pavement repair, there is another key area of parking lot duty, which is striping. Irrespective of the size of the parking lot, striping is essential. This directs the flow of the traffic, enhances safety, and keeps the restricted areas like the loading docks very clear. Also, updating the striping can help wear the asphalt layer more evenly, and also extend its life.
