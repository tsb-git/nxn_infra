Top 7 Factors to Consider in Professional Parking Lot Design Services

Business parking lots are always dynamic and are a reflection of the business structure. It also portrays the way people interact with the business. When it comes to designing a parking lot, there are a number of factors to consider making it extremely functional and efficient. From maximizing parking lot space to ensuring safety in the parking space, we have discussed the top seven factors to consider in designing professional parking lots.

Factors to consider while designing professional parking lots

There are several parking lot design considerations to ensure the best get out of parking spaces. Some of these standards include surface type, ramp lengths, slope requirements, ramp widths, turf areas, etc, to accommodate overhead obstructions such as power lines, low-hanging tree branches, etc.

1. Space optimization:

One of the most important aspects of parking lot design is space optimization. This involves the careful planning of the layout to maximize the number of parking spaces while still leaving scope for traffic flow and easy navigation. Utilizing the parking spaces and incorporating the one-way traffic patterns in the parking space will help to optimize the space and improve the overall capacity of the parking lot.

2. Safety and accessibility:

Ensuring the safety and accessibility of the parking lot is crucial for both drivers and pedestrians. This includes appropriate lighting, accessible parking spaces for people with disabilities, clearly marked crosswalks, etc. It is also important to consider the placement of speed bumps and stop signs to control the traffic and prevent accidents. The wide approaches and thick asphalt are the design elements we can consider for designing parking spaces for heavier vehicles carrying goods for resale.

3. Stormwater management and drainage:

When it comes to designing the parking lot, drainage is an important element to consider. Proper drainage ensures that the rainwater and other liquids can flow away from the surface of the parking lot, thus preventing the pooling of water and other potential damage. There are several techniques that we can incorporate into the design to optimize drainage. These techniques include the installation of permeable pavements using sloping surfaces to direct the water towards the drainage systems. By implementing an effective drainage system, we can improve the longevity and functionality of the parking lot while reducing the risk of water damage or flooding.

4.Landscaping and aesthetics:

While functionality is the key in designing the parking spaces, the aesthetics of the parking lot should never be overlooked. We can try incorporating low-maintenance landscaping elements such as shrubs, flowers, native trees, etc. Such elements will help improve the overall appearance of the parking lot, and also offer shade and mitigate heat absorption. Also, using materials such as stamped concrete, decorative pavers, etc., will add to the visual appeal and enhance the overall visual aesthetics of the parking lot.

5. Sustainability considerations:

In today’s world, sustainability is an important consideration to have in any civil design project. When it comes to designing the parking lot, several sustainable elements are to be incorporated. This includes the use of permeable pavements to reduce the stormwater runoff, installing the electric vehicle charging stations, incorporating solar panels to provide energy for lighting, to name a few. Designing sloped areas with native vegetation plantations is also becoming increasingly popular to help absorb excess stormwater in parking lots.

6. Appropriate lighting:

While designing the parking lot, special considerations should be made to ensure adequate lighting on both sides of the parking lanes. This will ensure that the vehicles are able to see the oncoming traffic. It is mandatory to install the light poles in both directions and ensure that drivers have adequate time to see the incoming vehicles as they exit the parking space. Appropriate lighting also ensures that pedestrians easily spot cars coming down the parking lane before crossing it.

7. Using appropriate signage in the parking lot:

It is mandatory to incorporate appropriate signage in the parking space to avoid confusion and to aid the efficient flow of traffic. The signs should be positioned in a way that the parking space is visible from the main entrance. The signage should not block visibility or access to the parking space. The sign boards should warn drivers against parking in the fire lanes and other areas meant for physically challenged individuals.

Bottom Line:

By incorporating these considerations in professional parking spaces, it is possible to create a functional, safe, and visually appealing parking space that meets the needs of both drivers and pedestrians. Irrespective of whether you are designing a new parking lot or are revamping an existing one, considering these factors will ensure an efficient and successful parking lot design.
